CS 466 Project : Alignments

About:

We were asked to implement an algorithm that we discussed in CS466: Introduction to Bioinformatics. Our team decided to recreate global, fitting, and local alignment using Flask as our primary framework.

Alignments:

A global alignment of two strings w and v involves an end to end comparison of each character in each string. Comparisons of character will result in a score based on a scoring matrix. The alignment with the maximum score would correspond to the global alignment.

A local alignment of two strings w and v involves comparing multiple global alignments on all substrings of w with all substrings of v and returning the maximum score from all these global alignments. A scoring matrix is once again used to determine a score for each character comparison.

A fitting alignment of two strings w and v involves comparing multiple global alignments on all substrings of v with string w and returning the maximum score from all these global alignments. Just like in Global alignment, a scoring matrix is used to determine a score for each character comparison.
